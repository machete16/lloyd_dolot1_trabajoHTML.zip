Our Coffee Beans

Robusta coffee beans
are the second most globally produced coffee bean. They are most popular in Europe, the Middle East and Africa.
The name given to this coffee bean highlights its profile perfectly, known for its strong and sometimes harsh flavor profile.
The Robusta coffee bean contains high levels of caffeine, making it more resilient compared to Arabica beans.

Arabica coffee beans
Arabica is the most common type of coffee bean, originating from Ethiopia. It is considered the first-ever coffee consumed.
The bean thrives in high elevation areas, such as Brazil, the world's foremost exporter of Arabica beans.

Libre coffee beans
These beans are native to the Amazon rainforest in Brazil and Peru. Known as "black coffee," they are grown in high-rainfall
and low-temperature areas, providing a source of energy and a non-alcoholic beverage option.

Excelsa beans
This bean type is a variant of Liberica and is grown primarily in Southeast Asia. It has a unique fruity flavor profile
with lower caffeine content.
